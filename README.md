Airline Customer Satisfaction Prediction
An end-to-end classification framework utilizing Binomial Logistic Regression to analyze passenger survey data, uncover key drivers of customer satisfaction, and formulate data-backed operational strategies for passenger retention.
Project Overview
Customer retention is a vital KPI in the highly competitive aviation industry. This project constructs a predictive classification pipeline using Python and Scikit-learn to determine whether a passenger will report a "Satisfied" or "Dissatisfied" experience. 
By pulling apart the mathematical coefficients of the trained Logistic Regression model, we translate statistical shifts (odds ratios) into actionable business intelligence—such as precisely mapping how infrastructure upgrades (e.g., in-flight Wi-Fi, seat comfort) mitigate the negative impacts of operational friction (e.g., departure delays).
Dataset & Feature Strategy
The analysis evaluates a mixture of demographic, operational, and subjective survey data:
Feature Engineering & Transformation Pipeline:
1. **Target Label Encoding**: The binary target variable (`satisfaction`) is structurally mapped:
   * `1` $\rightarrow$ **Satisfied** (Positive Class)
   * `0` $\rightarrow$ **Dissatisfied / Neutral** (Negative Class)
2. **Categorical Predictors**: Nominal categories (`Customer_Type`, `Class`) are transformed via **One-Hot Encoding**. The first category is explicitly dropped (`drop='first'`) to mitigate the risks of multi-collinearity (the dummy variable trap), establishing stable reference baselines.
3. **Numerical Scaling**: Continuous data (`Age`, `Flight_Distance`) and ordinal survey ratings (`Inflight_Wifi_Quality`, `Seat_Comfort`) are processed using a **StandardScaler**. Centering features to a mean of 0 and a standard deviation of 1 preserves model stability during gradient descent and permits direct coefficient magnitude comparisons.
Modeling Methodology
* **Algorithm**: Binomial Logistic Regression utilizing the `lbfgs` (Limited-memory Broyden–Fletcher–Goldfarb–Shanno) optimization solver.
* **Validation Split**: A **Stratified 80/20 Train/Test Split** ensures that the ratio of Satisfied to Dissatisfied passengers remains perfectly consistent across both subsets, protecting the model from evaluation bias.
* **Regularization**: L2 regularization (Ridge) is applied natively to penalize extreme coefficient weights and guarantee high generalization on unseen test data.
Evaluation Metrics & Reasoning
While **Accuracy** provides a general benchmark, optimizing airline operations requires evaluating true positive and false positive trade-offs. This project relies heavily on the following metrics:
* **Confusion Matrix**: Breaks down raw predictions into True Positives (TP), True Negatives (TN), False Positives (FP), and False Negatives (FN).
* **Precision ($\frac{TP}{TP + FP}$)**: Measures the accuracy of positive predictions. High precision ensures that if the model flags a passenger as "Satisfied", they truly are. This prevents the airline from misallocating retention budgets on passengers who are secretly unhappy.
* **Recall / Sensitivity ($\frac{TP}{TP + FN}$)**: Measures the percentage of actual satisfied customers captured. High recall means the model is excellent at identifying satisfied customers and minimizing False Negatives (dissatisfied passengers who were incorrectly classified as happy).
Interpretation of Coefficients (Drivers of Satisfaction)
Logistic Regression allows us to look past simple predictions and calculate the **Odds Ratio** for each operational lever by calculating the exponential of the model's coefficients ($e^{\beta}$):
* **Positive Drivers ($e^{\beta} > 1$)**: Features like `Inflight_Wifi_Quality` and `Seat_Comfort` yield high positive log-odds. For every standard deviation increase in these scores, the odds of a passenger being highly satisfied multiply by their respective odds ratio.
* **Negative Drivers ($e^{\beta} < 1$)**: Operational failures like `On_time_Departure_Delay` pull down satisfaction. This allows management to understand exactly how many minutes of delay it takes to wipe out the positive sentiment generated by a comfortable seat.
* **Baseline Benchmarks**: One-hot encoded features isolate class disparities (e.g., how much lower the baseline satisfaction odds are for `Class_Eco` compared to the reference baseline of `Class_Business`).
Strategic Business Insights
* **Targeted Capital Expenditure**: If the odds ratio of `Inflight_Wifi_Quality` outperforms `Seat_Comfort`, data justifies prioritizing digital infrastructure upgrades over physical seat retrofitting to maximize overall fleet satisfaction.
* **Service Recovery Thresholds**: By evaluating the drag coefficient of departure delays, the operations team can establish an automated threshold (e.g., >25-minute delay) to trigger automated compensation, proactively protecting customer lifetime value before surveys are even sent.
